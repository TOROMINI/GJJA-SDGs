Why we need this?

1.  Survival and Safety of Life Forms: Climate change has a significant impact on Earth's ecosystems

 and biodiversity. Rising temperatures, extreme weather events, and sea-level rise can destabilize

 ecosystems and put species at risk of extinction. Our lives and the resources we depend on for

 survival, such as food, water, and air, can be threatened.


2.  Infrastructure and Economic Impacts: Extreme climate events can cause significant damage to

 infrastructure. Changes in rainfall patterns, droughts, floods, coastal erosion, and more can damage

 roads, bridges, buildings, and result in substantial economic losses. Additionally, decreased crop

 yields or destruction of crops due to climate change can lead to food shortages and price increases,

posing economic challenges.

solutions

1.Expansion of Renewable Energy: The production and use of fossil fuels are major contributors to

 greenhouse gas emissions. Therefore, actively promoting and expanding renewable energy sources

 such as solar, wind, and hydro power is crucial. Governments and businesses should encourage the

 deployment of renewable energy generation facilities, while individuals and companies should strive

 to adopt renewable energy selectively and improve energy efficiency.



2.Reduction of Greenhouse Gas Emissions: The industrial and transportation sectors are known to be

 significant sources of greenhouse gas emissions. To address this, specific measures are needed to

 reduce emissions in each sector. Businesses should pursue eco-friendly technologies and implement

 emission reduction measures in their production processes. Governments can support these efforts

 through environmental regulations and financial incentives. Additionally, individuals can contribute

 by opting for public transportation, using environmentally friendly modes of transportation like

 bicycles and walking, and reducing personal vehicle usage.
