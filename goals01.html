No Poverty
빈곤 퇴치

Why we need this?

Human Life and Health: Hunger is a severe condition resulting from malnutrition. Nutritional

 deficiencies have negative impacts on physical and mental development and can be life-threatening.

 Hunger affects both children and adults, leading to decreased bodily functions, weakened immune

 systems, and reduced resistance to diseases



Opportunities and Development: Hunger poses a significant barrier to individual and societal

 development. Nutritional deficiencies hinder children's growth and development and limit

 educational opportunities and potential. Additionally, hunger can impair productivity and create

 economic hardships, hampering personal and national economic progress.

solutions

Increasing Food Production: Increasing food production is crucial for addressing hunger

 Here are some approaches that can be adopted:


Agricultural Innovation and Support:

Introducing innovative agricultural technologies and improving agricultural productivity are

 important. Providing support programs that offer education and funding to farmers can foster

 sustainable agriculture and increase crop yields.


Water Management: Efficient water management is vital for increasing food production.

 Implementing water-saving techniques and improving irrigation systems can effectively utilize water

needed for crop growth.

Crop Diversification: Promoting crop diversification is important rather than relying on a single crop.

 Cultivating a variety of crops can enhance crop resilience, improve production stability, and ensure

 food supply diversity.

    Effects

    By eradicating poverty, the gap between the rich and the poor can be reduced,

    resulting in social stability.
